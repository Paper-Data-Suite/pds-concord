# Provisional Findings

* authors and subjects are different concepts;
* artifacts may have one, several, or no student subjects;
* physical writer, recorder, account owner, or file owner is not necessarily the sole author or contributor;
* groups, roles, responsibilities, and subteams may be session-specific and time-bounded;
* roles, responsibilities, tasks, and contributions are related but distinct concepts;
* responsibilities may change by stage, trial, milestone, or work period;
* tasks may depend on other tasks and require handoffs between students or subteams;
* assigned responsibility does not prove contribution or completion;
* blocked work is distinct from incomplete or neglected work;
* peer evidence and conflicting contribution claims require moderation;
* teacher observation forms may be multi-subject, multi-group, and multi-session;
* artifacts may document events, decisions, trials, milestones, handoffs, tests, troubleshooting, and revisions;
* decisions and plans may be revised or superseded without erasing earlier records;
* failed tests, prototypes, or approaches may still provide valuable evidence of collaboration;
* Concord must represent circumstances outside students’ control separately from performance;
* nonverbal, technical, organizational, testing, integration, support, and material contributions are valid collaborative evidence;
* external physical or digital work may be linked through cover sheets, attachment records, or location references;
* Concord should reference external digital products rather than inspect repositories, edit histories, or project files automatically;
* scores may draw from several artifacts;
* individual, subteam, and group scores may draw from overlapping evidence without becoming the same score;
* missing evidence is not negative evidence;
* extended writing belongs to Quillan;
* OMR belongs to ScoreForm;
* subject-specific worksheets remain outside Concord unless used as attached evidence;
* safety-related notes may be recorded, but formal incident management belongs elsewhere;
* Core’s QR contract must eventually support non-student, group, subteam, session, milestone, task, event, and multi-subject artifacts.
